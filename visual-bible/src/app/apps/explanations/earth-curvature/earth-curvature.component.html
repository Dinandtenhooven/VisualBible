Earth Curvature Error for Local Map Projection

When creating a map of a small area on Earth, we often treat the surface as flat. This is a good approximation for areas up to tens of kilometers in size. However, the Earth is curved, so this introduces some error in representing distances and shapes.

short conclusion

For a 15 km × 15 km area, the maximum height deviation from a flat plane is about 8.8 meters. The horizontal distance distortion is only a few millimeters. For most practical purposes, maps of areas under ~50 km can be treated as flat without significant error.

When mapping a small region of the Earth onto a flat square, we approximate the curved surface of the Earth as a plane. This introduces small geometric errors. Below is the mathematical explanation for estimating that error.

Earth Radius

We model the Earth as a sphere with radius:

R ≈ 6371 km

Height Deviation from Flat Plane

If a flat plane is tangent to the Earth at the center of the mapped region, the spherical surface rises slightly above that plane as you move away from the center.

The exact deviation at distance d from the center is:

 h(d) = R - √(R² - d²)

For small regions where d ≪ R, we can approximate:

 h(d) ≈ d² / (2R)

Example: 15 km × 15 km Area for the jerusalem region

Half width:

7.5 km

Distance from center to corner:

 d = √(7.5² + 7.5²)
 d ≈ 10.61 km

Maximum curvature deviation:

 h ≈ d² / (2R)
 h ≈ 8.8 meters

Horizontal Distance Distortion

Arc length on the sphere:

 s = Rθ

Chord distance on the flat plane:

 c = 2R sin(θ/2)

The difference between them is approximately:

 Δs ≈ s³ / (24R²)

For a 15 km distance:

 Δs ≈ 3–4 mm

General Formula for Any Map Size

If L is the side length of a square mapping region:

Maximum curvature deviation

h(L) ≈ L² / (4R)
    
Distance distortion

Δs(L) ≈ L³ / (24R²)

Conclusion

  • For a 15 km mapping region, curvature height error ≈ 8.8 m.
  • Horizontal distance distortion is only a few millimeters.
  • For most mapping tasks, areas under ~50 km can be treated as flat.
